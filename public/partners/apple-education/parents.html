Supporting Learning at Home

Practical tips and resources to help you support your child's educational technology use at home

Creating a Positive Learning Environment

Dedicated Learning Space

Set up a quiet, organized area where your child can focus on digital learning activities without distractions.

Structured Schedule

Establish regular times for educational technology use, balancing screen time with other activities.

Active Participation

Engage with your child's learning by asking about their projects and celebrating their digital creations.

Family Technology Agreement

Device Usage Rules
  • • Technology is for learning first
  • • Ask permission before downloading apps
  • • No devices during family meals
Digital Citizenship
  • • Be kind and respectful online
  • • Protect personal information
  • • Tell parents about uncomfortable situations
Balance & Wellness
  • • Take breaks every 30 minutes
  • • Maintain good posture
  • • Enjoy offline activities daily

Teaching Digital Citizenship

Help your child develop responsible digital habits and online safety awareness

Online Safety

  • Never share personal information
  • Report uncomfortable situations
  • Use strong, unique passwords
  • Think before clicking links

Digital Respect

  • Treat others with kindness online
  • Respect others' digital work
  • Give credit for others' ideas
  • Stand up against cyberbullying

Healthy Habits

  • Take regular breaks from screens
  • Maintain good posture
  • Balance screen time with physical activity
  • Get adequate sleep
